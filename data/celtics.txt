Season Overview
The Boston Celtics delivered another elite regular season, finishing with the best record in the Eastern Conference at 56–26. Their combination of championship experience, two-way star power, and elite coaching made them the East's most complete and feared team throughout the year.
Strengths
The Celtics excel on both ends of the floor. Their switching defense is among the best in the league, and their offensive system — built around spacing, movement, and isolation from star wings — is nearly impossible to consistently contain. Depth throughout the roster means they rarely have a weak spot to exploit.
Weaknesses
Boston's biggest vulnerability is complacency. As a veteran, experienced team, there are stretches of the regular season where they operate below full intensity. Against elite playoff defenses, their reliance on jump shooting can also become a liability when shots aren't falling.
Key Players
Jayson Tatum anchors the offense as a versatile, high-volume scorer capable of taking over games in the clutch. Jaylen Brown provides a complementary star with athleticism, shot creation, and defensive tenacity. Their supporting cast adds shooting, defense, and playoff-tested contributions up and down the rotation.
Coaching Analysis
Joe Mazzulla has fully established himself as one of the East's premier coaches. His system maximizes the team's switchability on defense while creating freedom and structure for his stars offensively. His in-game adjustments have grown noticeably sharper as his tenure has matured.
Playoff Outlook
The Celtics are the default Eastern Conference favorite. With experience, depth, coaching, and two top-15 players, they have every ingredient needed for a championship run. The road through the East is tougher than in recent years, but Boston remains the team everyone else has to go through.
